iPhone Apps

  • Legal Research and Scholarship

  • Bloomberg Law Reports

    Bloomberg BNA Law Reports deliver original, authoritative and timely reporting and analysis that include key legal, legislative, regulatory or business developments. Our extensive information gathering capabilities include veteran reporters on Capitol Hill and in the federal agencies, plus correspondents covering every state.

  • Fastcase

    Fastcase for the iPhone/iPad is a free legal research application, putting the American law library in the palm of your hand. Fastcase contains cases and statutes from all 50 states and from the federal government. You can search by citation, keyword (in Boolean or natural language), or browse statute collections.

  • HeinOnline

    Now you can have access to the world’s largest image-based legal research database, HeinOnline, on your iPhone/iPad. View the image-based PDF’s, access content by citation, browse by volume, navigate a volume with the electronic table of contents, and use full advanced searching techniques. HeinOnline account/authentication is required.

  • Lexis Advance

    Transform the way you conduct legal research on the go! Get your hands on the award-winning Lexis Advance® mobile app that was identified by eWEEK and the Consumer Electronics Association as the only innovative mobile app designed to support job efficiency for lawyers!

  • OpenRegs

    Take the Federal Register with you wherever you go! With the OpenRegs app for the iPhone and iPod Touch you can find recently issued notices of final and proposed rulemaking. Browse by agency or comment periods closing soon or recently opened. You can mark individual regulations with a star and then easily view a list of all your starred regs. Regulations can also be emailed to a friend or to yourself for later reference. Best of all, it's free!

  • OverDrive

    Borrow eBooks, audiobooks, and streaming video from your library using OverDrive on your iPad, iPhone, and iPod touch. More than 30,000 libraries worldwide offer titles from OverDrive, so download the app and find your next book today!

  • Social Science Research Network (SSRN)

    iSSRN is a must have for anyone interested in the social sciences and now, humanities. Social Science Research Network (SSRN) is devoted to the rapid worldwide dissemination of scholarly research.

  • WestlawNext

    Tap into the world's most advanced legal research system on-the-go. Access familiar WestlawNext features including, WestSearch™, KeyCite™, Folders, History, document notes and highlighting, browse database content and more -- all redesigned for the iPad multi-touch screen. WestlawNext account required.

  • Legal News

  • ABA Journal Daily News

    Read summaries of the latest legal news, updated as it happens throughout every business day. Plus find original news reports and analysis, and read in-depth monthly magazine articles, for free wherever you are. Readers also will find contests, galleries, questions, polls and lively comments.

  • The American Lawyer

    The nation’s leading legal monthly magazine now accessible through an Ipad app.

  • Legal Edge

    Stay on top of the latest legal developments that matter to you with updates, news alerts, and case filings from the nation’s legal professionals.

  • National Law Journal

    The National Law Journal stands alone as the weekly newspaper legal professionals rely on for the latest news and analysis.

  • ISCOTUSNow

    ISCOTUSnow provides detailed information on all U.S. Supreme Court cases accepted for review or decided by the Court in the current and immediately preceding Terms.

QnA Markup

QnA Markup is a simple computer language written for lawyers with little or no programming experience. It transforms blocks of text into interactive question and answer sessions (QnAs). These QnAs can be used as stand-alone expert systems or in the aid of rule-based document construction. The entire project is open source, and among other things, that means free. Consequently, QnA is ideal for lawyers and law students looking to turn themselves into attorney-coders. The main site displays an editor where you can author your own QnAs, but be warned, it doesn't look great on a phone. QnAs themselves, however, look quite nice. Check out the sample below.

This Site

Curated by David Colarusso on GitGub, with contributions from CPCS legal-tech interns Jodie Gurry and Vedika Mehera.

The pages found under http://ma-defenders.github.io/incourt/ "This Site" are a collection of materials the authors have found useful to have on hand in court. You'll find three flavours of content here:

  • good old fashioned links (including deep links) with citation;
  • resources re-formatted for mobile and served from This Site (either in the public domain, used with permission, or subject to fair use); and
  • interactive aids called guesstimators.

    Since This Site is optimized for mobile browsing, links aren't always depicted by blue underlined text. All pages on This Site, however, have the same black navigation bar at the top.

    A list of content sources can be found by clicking on the light-gray "i" button found in a page's navigation bar.

    Special thanks to Flickr user Manu_H's (Emmanuel Huybrechts) for his picture of the John Adams Courthouse. It can be found at the Wikimedia Commons and is used here under a Creative Commons Attribution 2.0 Generic license. Variations on the image are used in this page's Open Graph preview as well as its mobile icon.
